Context

What this is / provenance

What this is

Three locally-running applications that use large language models to build named, voiced advisory rooms for building, making, and research. The question driving them isn't "how do I make agents do more things" — it's closer to what the Gastown experiments gestured at, pointed differently: what if the room was for ideas? A writer's room. A good scrum. Something generative rather than extractive.

The voices are fictional, historical, imagined. They are made to have distinct gifts and genuine points of view. The best things come out when they're all in the room at once — agreeing, disagreeing, rambling, challenging, colliding on something worth playing with. That's the design.

Some of what's described here is further along than the repos suggest. The east/west collaboration process, the question of what good creative process actually feels like in this moment, and the playable archive idea behind the Secret-Cabinet all have more life than they currently have URLs. This page is a timestamp on when the tinkering started and where it was on a given day.

Part of a larger set of projects at racheldixon.com and the nascent Wrong Answers Game Studio.

Collaboration

Jim Toepel at funderdevelopment is the Pacific desk — NYC to LA, two studios, one shared case board. The meta-cabinet is one artifact of that collaboration, but the collaboration itself is the bigger experiment: a structured, documented, cross-coast creative process between two humans using AI-assisted workflows, actively trying to figure out what good actually feels like. It's generative, it's fun, and we don't see it demonstrated anywhere else quite like this. The wagscrum board is where that lives.

On the AI question

These rooms exist because putting a human face on a model — even a fictional one, even a historical one — changes what you get out of it. That is either a useful illusion or a real one.

Is it pure entertainment? This is a question we ask often, and honestly one we may never answer. The PC, the iPhone, and the connected web didn't solve it either. We're in the conversation knowing full well we won't determine the answer. The jury is still out. These are tinkerings and experiments, not theses — and even then, we'd probably lean toward tinkering.

The practice

How the rooms work

Collision room

A named group of distinct AI voices, each with a defined register and a genuine point of view. Not a panel of assistants — a writer's room. The desk takes what the room produces.

Voice, not chatbot

Each member has a specific character — their idiom, their preoccupations, their blind spots. Borges doesn't speak like Stringer Bell. That's the point.

The desk writes

The rooms are read-only. They propose, push back, surface. The human at the desk writes the actual response, citing whose angle they took.

The room compounds

Different minds with different gifts, in the same room at the same time, produce something none of them would have alone. Agree, disagree, ramble, cut each other off — something comes out the other side. That's the whole bet.

II.

The Journal Cabin'ét dossier-placard

Private journal annotation · Day One integration
Est. 2026 · flagship
11 voices · 2 tiers
github ↗

A local web application that reads private journal entries and produces a salon of glosses — responses from a board of fictional and historical voices, rendered as a broadside.

The conceit: a private edition of The Cabin'ét, printed for one. The entry is read aloud. The voices respond. The record is exported back to Day One.

The room has two tiers: a permanent Trio (always seated, always generative — they leave a door open to the next piece of writing) and a Rotation pool (excavation voices, weighted by what's in the entry). Visual register: broadside newspaper. Brooklyn. Live deliciously.

III.

The Secret-Cabin-et Novel research

Historical esotericists · A room outside of time
The Affected (WIP)
8 standing voices
github ↗

Research tool for a novel-in-progress. A local salon application that places historical esotericists in conversation with research notes, draft passages, and questions about the occult. The room exists outside time. Members don't remember previous meetings. No one knows they're being observed.

A document is read aloud. The lodge responds. Three rounds of genuine cross-talk — not parallel independent responses. Part of this is serious research for The Affected. Part of it is that watching Crowley and Waite go at each other is genuinely entertaining, and that matters too.

This room is also the earliest working instance of an idea first pitched in a Graduate Center seminar in fall 2020: that research archives should feel playable — serendipitous, alive, capable of surprise even when everything is indexed. It took five years and a novel to find the right container. Integrated with Day One via MCP; sessions export back to PreSeedings of the Secret Cabinet.

Exhibit A — the lodge on a research note

Secret-Cabin-et · illustrative composite
Source note: "Mathers translated the Book of the Sacred Magic of Abramelin in 1898. It requires six months of preparation, absolute chastity, and culminates in contact with the Holy Guardian Angel — your own higher self, or something else entirely. Crowley attempted it at Boleskine. He left before finishing."
Aleister Crowley
He left because he understood, finally, what the operation was for — not the Angel, which is merely the hook — but the revelation of the Dweller on the Threshold, your own accumulated filth made suddenly legible. Mathers was too frightened to say this plainly. Abramelin is a mirror operation. The light it calls forth illuminates what was already in the room. I left because I had seen enough. There is a difference between stopping and failing, though the view from outside is identical.
Arthur Edward Waite
This is precisely the kind of retrospective self-exculpation that renders Crowley's accounts so structurally unreliable as historical documents. The operation was not completed. That is a fact. What he experienced during the uncompleted attempt is unknowable; what he subsequently narrates about that experience is entirely consistent with a man who has spent forty years constructing an elaborate personal mythology to account for a single retreat. The distinction between the Dweller and the Angel exists in the architecture of the ritual. He abandoned the architecture before the distinction could become operative.
Teresa of Ávila
Both of you are describing the same room from opposite ends of the corridor and refusing to acknowledge the corridor exists. The operation fails — or stops — at precisely the moment when what is being sought becomes indistinguishable from what is doing the seeking. This is not a flaw in the design. This is the design. The mystics who completed analogous operations did not complete them by finishing. They completed them by ceasing to require completion. Six months of preparation is preparation for release of the question, not acquisition of the answer. Boleskine was cold. The cold is mentioned in every account. No one asks why.
Three rounds of cross-talk. Members disagree. The desk decides what to take. The fire is lit; the room remembers nothing.
