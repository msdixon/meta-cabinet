Research tool for a novel-in-progress. A local application that places historical esotericists in conversation in an imagined intellectual salon, interrogating research notes, draft passages, and questions about the occult. The room exists outside time. Members don't remember previous meetings. No one knows they're being observed.

A document is read aloud. A selected group is convened, and responds. Three rounds of genuine cross-talk — not parallel independent responses. Part of this is serious research for *The Affected, a novel in progress *that has historical secret society themes. Part of it is that watching Crowley and Waite go at each other is genuinely entertaining, and that matters too.

This room is also the earliest working instance of an idea first pitched in a Graduate Center seminar in fall 2020: that digital research can and should feel *playable* — serendipitous, alive, capable of surprise even when everything is indexed. Physical archives have this by default; digital archives lose the vibe and therefore, the joy of discovery. Information isn't just data; people feel something in a library stack's architecture and lockdown showed us the limitations of digital archival flatness. 

It took five years and a novel to find the right container. Integrated with Day One MCP and Obsidian; sessions can export back to a separate DayOne journal, *PreSeedings of the Secret Cabinet, *to standalone PDF, Ulysses, or Obsidian.
